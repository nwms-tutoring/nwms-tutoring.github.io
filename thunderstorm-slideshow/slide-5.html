So, what actually makes a thunderstorm happen?


A thunderstorm is a weather event characterized by lightning and thunder, often accompanied by heavy rain, strong winds, and sometimes hail or tornadoes. It forms when warm, moist air rises rapidly into cooler parts of the atmosphere, creating tall cumulonimbus clouds. Electrical charges build up within these clouds, leading to lightning, which in turn creates the shock waves that we hear as thunder.
But they're not as harmless as they seem! Thunderstorms can combine with each other and form larger more severe weather system, like a derecho or a tornado!
